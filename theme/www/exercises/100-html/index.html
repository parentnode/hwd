So lets talk a bit about structure and why its is so important, First of all most html documents typically consist of a "header" a "footer" and something in between, lets call the in between "content". Just look at source code of this page. Here you can see the div's within div's, and inside those p tags and inside those em tag and so on. Right "above" the "content div" is an empty "header div", and "below" the content is an "empty" "footer div". they all live on the same "level" if you will, or they are siblings. They have the same parent div with the "id = page". you can see that there is a body tag that has a sibling called head. These to children, body and head, have a parent called html. There you have the whole family. Do you notice a system here? Every time there is a child or children there is an indentation. Indentations happen when you press the tab button in a document. The tab sits on the very left side of your keyboard next to the letter Q. it makes 4 spaces in front of the letter behind it like so:

look at me I'm an indent example

Note that the "html" tag is not indented in the source code. That is convention. That is because everyone has agreed to let it sit on the same level as its children to save space. There is no need to move everything thing more to the right than needed. In that way all the div's in the html document has a better chance of staying inside the screen when you use the code. So you could actually write html without making indentation at all, it would just become very confusing. While we are at it remember that tags are always above each other. here is how to do it: <div class="outer_div"> <div class="Im_a_div" > <div class= "long" > <p> ;Im a long text that goes farrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrr note how all my text and my other tags inside are indented relative to my p tag. and I would like my programmer to se my end tag. <span>Im a span in a paragraph </span> </p> </div> <div class= "short" > <p>im a short text only one line, my</p> </div> </div> </div>

Note to span in the paragraph, is inside the p tag. Note also that the short line has p tags in the end and the front on the same line.
